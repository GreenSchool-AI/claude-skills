# Example 1 — Booking page gets visits but few bookings

**Scenario (synthetic).** A mobile dog-grooming service in Perth says: "Heaps of people land on my booking page from Instagram, but hardly anyone books. The page has all my services, prices, a long bio, photos, and a contact form with about ten fields. What's going wrong?"

---

## How the skill responds

**Quick context check:** the desired behaviour is *complete a booking*; audience is local pet owners coming warm from Instagram. That's enough to work.

**The models that fit here:**

### 1. Hick's Law + Paradox of Choice
Visitors face a wall of services and a 10-field form. More choices and more fields = slower decisions and more drop-off.
**Do this:** Lead with one primary action — "Book a groom" — and a short form (name, suburb, phone, dog size/breed). Move the full service menu below or onto a second step. Aim for 4 fields, not 10; every extra field costs you bookings.

### 2. Activation Energy
The first step feels big. Lower it.
**Do this:** Offer "Check my suburb & price in 20 seconds" as the entry point instead of "Fill in the booking form". A tiny, easy first action pulls people into the flow (foot-in-the-door).

### 3. Social Proof
Warm Instagram traffic still wants reassurance this is safe for their dog.
**Do this:** Put 2-3 short reviews and a "200+ Perth dogs groomed this year" figure right next to the button. Real numbers, real names/suburbs.

### 4. Regret Aversion / Risk Reversal
Booking a stranger to handle your pet is a regret-risk decision.
**Do this:** Add a simple guarantee — "Not happy with the groom? We'll make it right, no charge." Reassurance near the CTA reduces the fear that blocks the click.

**What I'd change first (this week):**
1. Cut the form to 4 fields and a single clear button.
2. Add 3 reviews + a count beside the button.
3. Add the one-line guarantee.
Then watch whether booking rate lifts before touching anything else (theory of constraints — fix the biggest blocker first).

**Kept ethical:** the "200+ dogs" figure must be true, and the reviews real.
