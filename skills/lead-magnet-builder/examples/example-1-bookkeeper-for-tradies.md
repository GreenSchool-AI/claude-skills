# Example 1 — Bookkeeper for tradies wants more leads

**Scenario (synthetic).** "I'm a bookkeeper specialising in tradies — sparkies, plumbers, builders. I want to grow an email list of tradies who might become clients. No idea what to give away or what to send them."

---

## What the skill produces

**Quick context:** audience = sole-trader and small-crew tradies; goal = qualified leads; the recurring pain (from enquiries) = tax-time panic and messy receipts. Consideration-stage magnet that shows competence.

### 1. Lead magnet recommendation
**Format:** Checklist (one page). **Topic:** "The Tradie's Tax-Time Receipt Checklist — the 12 things the ATO expects, and the 6 deductions tradies miss." Low effort (2 hrs), high perceived value, directly adjacent to the paid service.

### 2. Content outline
- The 12 records to keep (invoices, vehicle logbook, tool purchases, subbie payments, etc.).
- 6 commonly-missed deductions (work vehicle, tools under the instant asset write-off, protective gear, union/licence fees, home-office admin, training).
- A "shoebox to sorted in 20 minutes" mini-process.
- One line: when it's worth handing this to a bookkeeper.

### 3. Opt-in page copy
**Headline options:**
- A: "The tax-time checklist every Aussie tradie should have on the ute." *(plain-spoken, audience-specific)*
- B: "Stop dreading the ATO. 12 records + 6 deductions tradies miss." *(pain + specifics)*
- C: "Quoting all day, panicking at tax time?" *(question naming the pain)*

**What's inside:** the 12 must-keep records · 6 deductions you're probably missing · a 20-minute clean-up process · how to never lose a receipt again.
**Button:** "Send me the checklist" (not "Submit").
**Reassurance:** "No spam — just the checklist and the odd useful tip. Unsubscribe anytime."
**Fields:** first name + email only.

### 4. Delivery email (send immediately)
**Subject options:** "Here's your tradie tax checklist" · "[First name], your checklist's inside" · "12 records the ATO wants from you"

> Hey [First name],
> Here's your Tradie Tax-Time Receipt Checklist — [download link]. Save it to your phone so it's there on site.
> If you only do one thing today: start the vehicle logbook (page 1). It's the deduction tradies most often lose, and it takes two minutes to set up.
> Reply and tell me your trade — I'll point you to the deductions that matter most for your line of work.
> Cheers,
> [Name], [Business]

### 5. Nurture outline (~2 weeks)
1. **Deliver** (now) — above.
2. **Quick win** (day 2) — "The deduction most [sparkies/plumbers] miss" with a worked AUD example.
3. **Proof** (day 5) — short story: a tradie who got $X back after sorting records.
4. **Soft offer** (day 8) — "Want me to just handle this?" → one CTA to book a 15-min chat.
Exit when they book or reply.

**Kept honest:** deduction specifics should be checked against current ATO rules; the refund story must be real.
