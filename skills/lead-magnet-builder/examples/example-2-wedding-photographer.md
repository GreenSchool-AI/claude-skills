# Example 2 — Wedding photographer wants to capture leads earlier

**Scenario (synthetic).** "I'm a wedding photographer in Perth. Couples find me on Instagram but most aren't ready to book for months. I want their email so I stay top of mind — but a 'newsletter' is a hard sell."

---

## What the skill produces

**Quick context:** audience = newly-engaged couples, early planning; goal = capture early, nurture to enquiry; "newsletter" has no pull, so lead with a planning tool. Awareness/consideration-stage magnet.

### 1. Lead magnet recommendation
**Format:** Template. **Topic:** "The Stress-Free Wedding-Day Timeline Template — a photographer's hour-by-hour plan so you actually enjoy the day." A template has high perceived value, is instantly useful, and positions the photographer as the calm expert — adjacent to booking.

### 2. Content outline
- A fill-in timeline from getting-ready to last dance, with realistic photo windows.
- "Golden hour" timing notes for WA seasons (so portraits aren't rushed).
- Buffer-time rules couples always forget.
- A short "what your photographer needs from you" checklist.

### 3. Opt-in page copy
**Headline options:**
- A: "The wedding-day timeline that keeps the day calm (from a photographer who's shot 200+)."
- B: "Plan a wedding day you actually enjoy — hour by hour."
- C: "Worried the day will be a blur?"

**What's inside:** an editable hour-by-hour timeline · golden-hour timing for WA · the buffers couples forget · what your photographer needs.
**Button:** "Send me the timeline."
**Reassurance:** "No spam, just genuinely useful planning. Unsubscribe anytime."
**Fields:** first name + email (and optional wedding date — it qualifies and lets you time follow-ups).

### 4. Delivery email (send immediately)
**Subject options:** "Your wedding-day timeline's inside" · "[First name], here's your timeline template" · "The plan for a calm wedding day"

> Hi [First name],
> Congratulations! Here's your Wedding-Day Timeline Template — [link]. It's editable, so make it yours.
> Start with the golden-hour box: lock your portrait time to the light and everything else falls into place.
> Reply with your date and venue and I'll send timing tips specific to your location.
> Warmly,
> [Name], [Studio]

### 5. Nurture outline
1. **Deliver** (now) — above.
2. **Quick win** (day 3) — "The 15 minutes most couples forget to budget for" (and why it ruins the photos).
3. **Proof** (day 7) — a short, real couple's story + a few images.
4. **Soft offer** (day 12) — "If you're still choosing a photographer, here's how I work" → one CTA to view availability.
Exit when they enquire. If a wedding date was given, time a check-in ~9-12 months out.

**Kept honest:** the "200+" figure and the couple's story must be real.
