# Example 1 — "A Quick Website Refresh" (AU SMB enquiry)

**Scenario (synthetic).** An AU small business emails a freelance web designer: "Hi, we just need a quick refresh of our website — nothing major, should be pretty simple. Can you send us a quote?"

---

## OUTPUT (what the skill returns)

### 1. Diagnostic questions (15-20) — ask before quoting
- What does "refresh" mean to you — new look, new content, new structure, or all three?
- How many pages, and which ones?
- Do you have the content (copy, images) ready, or is that part of the job?
- What platform is the site on now (WordPress, Squarespace, Shopify, Wix, custom)?
- Who owns the hosting and domain logins?
- What single outcome must this refresh achieve (more enquiries, look current, mobile-friendly)?
- Who signs off, and who else has opinions?
- What's the timeline, and is anything driving it?
- Do you have a budget range in mind?
- Will you need ongoing updates after launch?
- Brand guidelines provided, or are we creating them?
- Any SEO or analytics requirements?
- Any integrations (booking, payments, CRM)?
- Examples of sites you like?
- Accessibility requirements (relevant for some AU sectors)?

### 2. Request decomposed into deliverables
- In scope (pending answers): visual refresh of up to [N] existing pages on the current platform; mobile-responsiveness check; minor copy edits.
- Out of scope unless agreed: new copywriting, new photography, platform migration, new functionality / integrations, ongoing maintenance.
- Success criteria: agree with the client (e.g. "looks current and works on mobile").
- Dependencies: client supplies content, brand assets, and logins by [date].

### 3. Risks and red flags
- "Quick", "simple", "nothing major" + "send a quote" with no detail = a classic scope-creep setup.
- No budget mentioned.
- "Refresh" is undefined — could be a coat of paint or a rebuild.
- Content is probably not ready (the hidden time sink).

### 4. Scope-creep risk areas
- Copy and images "while you're in there".
- "Can you also just add…" (booking, blog, shop).
- Endless revision rounds. Contract phrasing: "Includes two revision rounds; further rounds billed at $[rate]/hour."

### 5. Three pricing structures (AU context)
- Fixed price — only after scope is locked; safest for the client, riskiest for you while scope is vague. Indicative AU range for a genuine multi-page refresh: ~$1,500-5,000+ AUD by page count and content work.
- Day rate — good while scope is fuzzy; ~$600-1,200 AUD/day is a common AU freelance range.
- Paid discovery first — a small scoping fee to define the work, then a fixed quote. Best fit here, given the vagueness.
GST: add 10% once you're over the $75k AUD threshold.

### 6. Contract structure outline
- Payment: 50% deposit, 50% on launch (or 30/40/30 for larger jobs).
- Two revision rounds included; extra rounds billed hourly.
- Change-order process for anything outside the agreed deliverables.
- GST stated on the invoice.
- Cancellation: deposit non-refundable once work starts.

[scope summary]
