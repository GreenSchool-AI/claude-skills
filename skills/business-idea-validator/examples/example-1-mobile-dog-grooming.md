# Example 1 — Mobile Dog Grooming, Sunshine Coast

**Scenario (synthetic).** "I want to start a mobile dog grooming business on the Sunshine Coast. I've groomed for a salon for five years and want to go solo with a fitted-out van."

---

## OUTPUT (what the skill returns)

### 1. Verdict
Worth testing. Demand and your experience are real; the make-or-break is van fit-out cost versus how many grooms you can realistically do per day.

### 2. Market viability snapshot (AU)
- Market: pet ownership is high on the Sunshine Coast, with a large, time-poor dog-owning demographic. Mobile grooming is an established, growing niche.
- Saturation: amber — several mobile operators already work the Coast; differentiation matters.
- Demand signals: steady searches for "mobile dog groomer [suburb]"; salon waitlists; a convenience premium for at-home service.
- AU competitors: Aussie Pooch Mobile (franchise) plus local solo operators advertising on Facebook — check who already covers your suburbs.

### 3. ICP + value proposition
ICP: busy and less-mobile dog owners in your target suburbs who'd rather not drive to a salon. Value proposition: "Salon-quality grooming in your driveway, booked in 30 seconds." AI edge: instant booking, automated reminders, and quick on-brand social posts — so your time goes on grooming, not admin.

### 4. Three MVP options
- Start with your own car + portable equipment for a fortnight before committing to a van fit-out.
- Rent or borrow a fitted van for a month to test realistic daily capacity.
- Run a "founding clients" round of 10 regulars at a set price to prove repeat demand.

### 5. AUD pricing strategy
Per-groom pricing, tiered by dog size and coat. Realistic Sunshine Coast range is roughly $80-150 AUD per groom; mobile convenience supports the upper half. Confirm against 3-4 local competitors before setting.

### 6. Top 5 risks + mitigations
- Van fit-out cost (high) → test demand before financing a full fit-out.
- Capacity ceiling — only so many grooms a day (high) → cluster bookings by suburb to cut travel.
- Fuel and travel time eating margin (med) → minimum booking value + a tight service radius.
- Water and power on site (med) → self-contained tank + generator; confirm before each job.
- Seasonality (med) → pre-book recurring 6-8 week cycles to smooth income.

### 7. AU regulatory checklist
ABN required. GST registration once turnover passes $75k AUD. Public liability insurance is essential (working at clients' homes with animals). Check Sunshine Coast Council rules for mobile businesses and any water-discharge requirements. No specific grooming licence in QLD, but verify the current council position. See business.gov.au for setup.

### 8. Validate before you spend
Experiments: (1) post in three Sunshine Coast suburb Facebook groups and count enquiries in a week; (2) take 10 pre-bookings at full price before buying a van; (3) run a $50 AUD local Facebook ad to a simple booking page and measure cost per booking.
Mom-Test questions (behavioural): "When did you last get your dog groomed, and how did you book?", "What did it cost and what annoyed you about it?", "Walk me through the last time you couldn't get an appointment." (…plus seven more in the same style — never "would you use a mobile groomer?")
Kill criteria: if you can't get 10 paying pre-bookings within four weeks, or cost-per-booking from ads exceeds your margin, pause before financing the van.

### 9. Your first week
Confirm your service suburbs; price-check four competitors; set up an ABN and a simple booking page; post in two local Facebook groups; line up three founding clients from your salon contacts.
