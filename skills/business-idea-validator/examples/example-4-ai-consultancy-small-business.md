# Example 4 — AI Consultancy for Small Business

**Scenario (synthetic).** "I've gotten good at using AI tools and want to start a consultancy helping small businesses adopt AI — setting up workflows, training their staff, that kind of thing. Is there a real business in this?"

---

## OUTPUT (what the skill returns)

### 1. Verdict
Worth testing, with a warning: the idea is sound and demand is real, but "AI consultant" is a crowded, noisy label right now. Your make-or-break isn't capability — it's positioning and proof. Niche down and lead with outcomes, not "AI", or you'll blur into a busy field.

### 2. Market viability snapshot (AU)
- Market: a very large base of AU small businesses, most aware they "should be using AI" but unsure how — strong latent demand.
- Saturation: amber-to-red on the generic "AI consultant" positioning; green if you own a specific niche (e.g. "AI for AU real-estate agencies").
- Demand signals: SMBs asking peers how to start; spend shifting from curiosity to "make my team faster"; but also heavy scepticism after being burned by hype.
- AU competitors: a flood of solo "AI consultants" on LinkedIn, plus established IT/digital agencies adding AI services and the big consultancies chasing larger clients — your edge is being affordable, local, and niche.

### 3. ICP + value proposition
ICP: owner-operated AU businesses of 5-30 staff in one chosen vertical, drowning in admin and ready to pay for time back. Value proposition: lead with the outcome — "Cut your team's admin time by [X] hours a week" — not the technology. AI is the how, not the pitch. Here AI is the product itself, so your credibility is the asset; case studies beat claims.

### 4. Three MVP options
- Run one paid pilot with a single business in your chosen niche and document the before/after in hours and dollars.
- Offer a fixed-scope "AI quick-start" — a half-day audit plus two workflows set up — as a productised entry offer.
- Teach a paid workshop to a local business group; it validates demand and generates leads at the same time.

### 5. AUD pricing strategy
Value-based or fixed-scope packages, not hourly — SMBs fear an open-ended consultant meter. A productised audit might sit around $1,500-3,000 AUD; a quick-start project higher. Anchor the price to hours saved, not effort spent. Avoid undercharging to win early logos — it's hard to raise later.

### 6. Top 5 risks + mitigations
- Generic positioning lost in the noise (high) → pick one vertical and own it before broadening.
- No proof / no track record (high) → run a discounted pilot purely to earn a measurable case study.
- Tool churn — recommending tools that change or vanish (med) → favour stable, recognised platforms; sell the outcome, not a specific tool.
- Clients expecting magic, then disappointed (med) → set scope and realistic before/after expectations up front.
- Data privacy and client confidentiality (med) → clear handling practices; note Australian Privacy Principles where client data is involved.

### 7. AU regulatory checklist
ABN required. GST registration once turnover passes $75k AUD. No specific licence to consult, but professional indemnity insurance is strongly advised given you're advising on business operations. If you handle client customer data, be mindful of the Privacy Act / Australian Privacy Principles. A simple services agreement (scope, liability, IP) per client is worth having. See business.gov.au and oaic.gov.au. (General information, not legal advice.)

### 8. Validate before you spend
Experiments: (1) offer a paid pilot to one business in your target niche and measure hours saved; (2) post a specific outcome ("I saved a [niche] business 8 hours/week — here's how") and count enquiries; (3) run one paid workshop and track how many attendees ask about follow-on work.
Mom-Test questions (behavioural): "What's the most time-consuming admin task your team does weekly?", "Have you tried any AI tools — what happened?", "When did you last pay someone to improve how your business runs?" (…plus seven more — never "would you hire an AI consultant?")
Kill criteria: if a paid pilot doesn't produce a result worth a testimonial, or specific-outcome posts generate no enquiries over a month, sharpen the niche before investing further.

### 9. Your first week
Choose one vertical; write a single outcome-led offer; line up one pilot client (even discounted) to earn a case study; set up an ABN and a one-page services agreement; and draft the before/after metrics you'll measure.
