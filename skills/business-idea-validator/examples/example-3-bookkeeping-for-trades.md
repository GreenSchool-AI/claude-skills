# Example 3 — Bookkeeping Business for Trades

**Scenario (synthetic).** "I've done the books for my partner's electrical business for years and I'm good at it. I want to start a bookkeeping business for tradies — electricians, plumbers, builders — who hate paperwork. I'd use AI to speed things up."

---

## OUTPUT (what the skill returns)

### 1. Verdict
Worth testing — but one regulatory point decides how you can operate. Trades genuinely hate admin and will pay to offload it; the catch is that charging for BAS work requires registration (see the checklist). Get that clear first, then it's a solid, low-capital service business.

### 2. Market viability snapshot (AU)
- Market: hundreds of thousands of sole-trader and small trade businesses in Australia, most paperwork-averse and time-poor — a deep, steady niche.
- Saturation: amber — plenty of bookkeepers, but few who specialise in trades and speak the language of job costing, progress claims, and subbie payments.
- Demand signals: tradies routinely late on BAS; accountants charging premium rates for work a bookkeeper can do; constant "good bookkeeper?" posts in trade Facebook groups.
- AU competitors: local independent bookkeepers, franchise networks (e.g. First Class Accounts), and the tradie's own accountant — your edge is trade specialisation and responsiveness.

### 3. ICP + value proposition
ICP: sole-trader and small trade businesses (1-5 staff) doing their own books badly at 9pm on a Sunday. Value proposition: "Trade-specialist bookkeeping that keeps you compliant and paid faster — so you can put the laptop down." AI edge is real here: receipt capture and auto-coding (Dext/Hubdoc into Xero), bank-feed reconciliation, and automated invoice chasing cut the manual hours sharply — but AI doesn't replace the BAS-agent judgement you're being paid for.

### 4. Three MVP options
- Take on 3-5 founding clients from your partner's trade network at a set monthly fee before building any "business".
- Offer a one-off "catch-up and clean-up" package (a quarter of messy books tidied) as a low-commitment entry point.
- Standardise on one platform (Xero) plus one receipt tool and run your first clients through it to prove your time-per-client.

### 5. AUD pricing strategy
Fixed monthly packages, tiered by transaction volume and whether BAS is included — roughly $200-600 AUD/month for a small trade business; catch-up jobs priced per quarter. Monthly retainers beat hourly: they reward your AI-driven efficiency instead of penalising it.

### 6. Top 5 risks + mitigations
- Operating outside BAS-agent registration (high) → resolve registration before charging for any BAS work (see checklist); until then, scope work to data entry only.
- Pricing by the hour and being punished for being fast (med) → fixed-fee packages.
- Client concentration via one network (med) → diversify beyond your partner's contacts early.
- Messy client data eating your margin (med) → onboarding clean-up fee + a required receipt-capture app.
- Professional liability (low-med) → professional indemnity insurance.

### 7. AU regulatory checklist
ABN required. **To provide BAS services for a fee — preparing or lodging BAS, or advising on GST/PAYG — you must be a registered BAS agent with the Tax Practitioners Board (TPB)**, which requires a relevant qualification (e.g. Cert IV in Accounting and Bookkeeping) plus supervised experience and professional indemnity insurance. General data-entry bookkeeping that doesn't touch BAS doesn't require it, but most trade work will. GST registration once your own turnover passes $75k AUD. Consider membership of a body like the ICB or the Institute of Public Accountants. See tpb.gov.au and business.gov.au. (General information, not tax advice — confirm your situation with the TPB.)

### 8. Validate before you spend
Experiments: (1) offer a catch-up package to three tradies in a local trade group and count takers in two weeks; (2) time yourself doing one full month for one client with your AI stack to learn real hours-per-client; (3) ask five tradies what they currently pay their accountant and for what.
Mom-Test questions (behavioural): "Walk me through how you did your last BAS.", "When did you last pay an accountant or bookkeeper, and what for?", "What happened the last time your books were late?" (…plus seven more — never "would you hire a bookkeeper?")
Kill criteria: if you can't sign three paying clients in six weeks, or your real time-per-client makes the package unprofitable, rework the offer before scaling.

### 9. Your first week
Confirm your BAS-agent registration path with the TPB; set up an ABN; pick your Xero + receipt-capture stack; write one fixed-fee package; and line up two founding clients from the trade network for a catch-up job.
