# Example 2 — Cafe in Brisbane CBD

**Scenario (synthetic).** "I want to open a cafe in Brisbane CBD. I love coffee and have always dreamed of my own place."

---

## OUTPUT (what the skill returns)

### 1. Verdict
Probably not — at least not as a first business framed this way. Brisbane CBD cafes are saturated, capital-heavy, and low-margin; a love of coffee isn't an edge here.

### 2. Market viability snapshot (AU)
- Market: large weekday foot traffic, but hybrid work has thinned CBD weekday demand and weekends are quiet.
- Saturation: red — a cafe on nearly every corner, many well established.
- Demand signals: strong but heavily contested; loyalty is local and hard to shift.
- AU competitors: established independents plus major coffee chains holding prime corners on better leases than a newcomer will get.

### 3. ICP + value proposition
ICP: CBD office workers on weekday mornings. The problem: no clear reason for them to switch from their current cafe. Without a sharp differentiator (location, specialty niche, speed), the proposition is "another good cafe" — not enough. AI doesn't change the core economics here.

### 4. Three MVP options (test before signing a lease)
- Run a weekend market coffee cart for 6-8 weeks to test product and stamina.
- Do a pop-up in an existing venue's quiet hours.
- Cater office meetings per event to learn the customer without a fixed lease.

### 5. AUD pricing strategy
Coffee is price-anchored (~$5-6 AUD a cup in Brisbane); you have little pricing power. Margins live or die on rent and wastage, not price.

### 6. Top 5 risks + mitigations
- Lease cost and lock-in (high) → don't sign a CBD lease until a cart or pop-up proves demand.
- Fit-out capital ($150k+ AUD) (high) → start asset-light; lease equipment.
- Thin weekday-only demand (high) → count actual foot traffic by hour before committing.
- Staffing and penalty rates (med) → model award wages honestly.
- Competition on better sites (high) → only proceed with a genuine differentiator.

### 7. AU regulatory checklist
ABN; GST (you'll pass $75k AUD easily). Food business licence and a food-safety supervisor via Brisbane City Council. Council fit-out and food-premises approvals. Public liability insurance. Fair Work award rates for staff — penalty rates matter. See business.gov.au and Brisbane City Council.

### 8. Validate before you spend
Experiments: (1) run a weekend coffee cart and track cups sold and repeat faces; (2) stand outside your target site 7-9am for a week and count real foot traffic; (3) cater three office meetings and measure reorder rate.
Mom-Test questions (behavioural): "Where do you buy coffee on a workday, and why there?", "When did you last try a new cafe — what made you switch?", "Walk me through your morning coffee routine." (…plus seven more — never "would you come to my cafe?")
Kill criteria: if the cart can't clear its costs over 6-8 weekends, or foot-traffic counts don't support the numbers, don't sign the lease.

### 9. Your first week
Validate, don't commit. Price a weekend market stall; map three potential sites and count foot traffic; build a simple cost model (rent + wages + wastage) to see the real margin.
