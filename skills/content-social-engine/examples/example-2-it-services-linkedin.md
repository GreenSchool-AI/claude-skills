# Example 2 — IT support firm that wants leads, not likes

**Scenario (synthetic).** "We're an Adelaide managed-IT provider for small businesses (10-50 staff). I want LinkedIn to bring in enquiries, but I don't want to post cringe. What do we talk about?"

---

## What the skill produces

**Quick context:** B2B services; goal = qualified enquiries from SME owners/office managers; platform = LinkedIn (primary), with posts repurposed to a monthly email; time = ~2 hrs/week. Lead searchable-adjacent authority + proof.

### 1. Content pillars (4)
- **Plain-English education** (35%) — security, backups, and IT risk explained without jargon.
- **Proof** (25%) — anonymised client situations, before/after, "what we found".
- **Opinion/insight** (25%) — where SMEs waste IT spend, vendor myths, what "good" looks like.
- **The team & how we work** (15%) — response times, the people, the process. (Promo ~minimal.)

### 2. Starter plan (sample of 7)
- "The $0 security fix every small business skips" (education, awareness) — hook: *"It costs nothing and almost no one does it."*
- "We audited a 30-person firm. Here's what we found." (proof, consideration) — hook: *"Three things were quietly broken."*
- "Most SMEs overpay for IT in the same two ways" (opinion) — hook: *"It's not the hardware."*
- "MFA in plain English (and why your insurer now asks)" (education).
- "What a 2-hour outage actually costs a 20-person office" (insight, decision).
- "How we respond when something breaks at 4:55pm Friday" (process, proof).
- "Backups you think you have vs backups you can restore" (education).

### 3. Two posts written
**Education post:** "The $0 security fix every small business skips: turn on multi-factor authentication. No new software, no cost, and it blocks the vast majority of account break-ins. If your team logs into email or accounting from a browser, do this today. Want the 10-minute rollout steps? Comment 'MFA'."
**Proof post (carousel outline):** "We audited a 30-person Adelaide firm. Slide 1: the brief. 2-4: three quiet risks (shared admin password, no offsite backup, ex-staff still had access). 5: what we changed. 6: the lesson — most risk is mundane, not movie-villain. DM 'audit' for our free checklist."

### 4. Weekly rhythm (~2 hrs, batched)
- **Tue** education post. **Thu** proof or opinion post. Reply to every comment within the first hour. Monthly: stitch the best 3-4 posts into an email to existing clients/prospects.

### 5. Idea bank
Phishing examples (redacted) · "is your domain pretending to be you?" · cloud vs on-prem in plain English · the offboarding checklist · why cheap IT gets expensive · a client's worst-day-saved story · EOFY tech write-off basics · what to ask before signing an IT contract.

**Kept honest:** client stories anonymised and used with permission; no fear-mongering or made-up stats; any claim about insurer/regulatory requirements checked before posting.
