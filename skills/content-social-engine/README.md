# Content & Social Engine

A free Claude Skill by AI Courses by Green School that helps an Australian small business work out what to post, turn it into ready-to-use social content, and never run out of ideas — without a full-time content team. Decide → Post → Refill.

## Who it's for

Australian small business owners, solo operators, and small teams who know they "should be posting" but get stuck on what to make, how to say it, and how to keep it up.

## What you get

A plan a busy owner can actually sustain:

1. **Content pillars** — the 3-5 themes your business should be known for.
2. **A starter plan** — specific post ideas tagged by platform, format, and hook.
3. **Posts written for you** — a few captions and a video script, ready to paste.
4. **A weekly rhythm** — what to post where, sized to the time you have.
5. **An idea bank** — so you're never staring at a blank screen.

## How to use it (non-technical)

1. Open Claude (claude.ai or the desktop app) with this skill added (see "Installing the skill").
2. Tell it what you do and where your customers are — "I run a café in Newcastle and use Instagram and Facebook".
3. Answer a question or two, then post what it gives you and reuse the idea bank.

You don't need to name the skill — Claude recognises a content strategy, "what should I post", social media, or content-ideas request.

## Installing the skill

Full per-platform steps are in the repository's [INSTALL.md](https://github.com/GreenSchool-AI/claude-skills/blob/main/INSTALL.md).

**The short version:**

1. **[⬇️ Download the skill (ZIP)](https://github.com/GreenSchool-AI/claude-skills/raw/main/skills/content-social-engine/content-social-engine.zip)** — one ready-to-upload file.
2. In Claude, turn on **Code execution and file creation** under **Settings → Capabilities** (one-time).
3. Go to **Customize → Skills**, click **"+" → Create skill → Upload a skill**, and upload the ZIP.
4. Toggle it on, start a new chat, and tell it about your business and platforms.

## What it won't do

It won't help you buy followers, run engagement pods, or post undisclosed paid partnerships — those breach platform rules and Australian Consumer Law / ACCC disclosure rules. It plans and writes; you (or your scheduler) post.
