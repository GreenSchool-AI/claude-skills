# Example 1 — Declining Extra Workload Upward (Mode A)

**Scenario (synthetic).** A mid-level finance manager at an ASX-listed company needs to push back on extra work piled on during EOFY (End of Financial Year) by the CFO (Chief Financial Officer), without sounding insubordinate. Channel: email.

---

## INPUT

**Context:** Writing to the CFO during the EOFY crunch. Already at capacity. Needs to decline an ad-hoc analysis without burning the relationship.

**Draft (as supplied):**

> Hi [CFO],
> I just wanted to quickly reach out because I really think I might not be able to do the extra Q4 variance analysis you mentioned, as I'm honestly already pretty swamped with the EOFY close and a few other things, so I'm just a bit worried it might not get done properly. Sorry about this! Let me know what you think.
> Thanks so much,

---

## OUTPUT (what the skill returns)

### 1. Quick assessment
Your draft is 78 words and apologises twice for something reasonable. The versions below are ~45 words, hold the line, and offer a path — no apology needed.

### 2. Three versions

**Formal**
Subject: Q4 variance analysis — sequencing with EOFY close
Hi [CFO],
I want to make sure the Q4 variance analysis is done properly. With the EOFY close due [date], I can start it on [date], or hand part of it to [name] this week. Which would you prefer?
Kind regards, [Name]

**Neutral**
Hi [CFO],
Happy to take on the Q4 variance analysis. To do it well alongside the EOFY close, I can start [date], or bring [name] in now to run it in parallel. Your call on priority.
Cheers, [Name]

**Friendly**
Hi [CFO],
Can do the Q4 variance — I just need to slot it around the EOFY close. I'd start [date], or loop [name] in now if you need it sooner. What works?
Cheers, [Name]

### 3. Subject line options
- Direct: "Q4 variance analysis — sequencing with EOFY close"
- Curiosity: "Quick call on Q4 variance timing"
- Action-required: "Q4 variance — need a priority call by [date]"

### 4. AU workplace culture notes
- The draft apologises twice. In AU corporate culture, re-sequencing reasonable work doesn't warrant an apology — it reads as low status. Removed.
- "Just", "really", and "honestly" are softeners that weaken the message. Cut.
- Offering options (start later / delegate) turns a "no" into a priority decision for the CFO — far stronger than a flat refusal.

### 5. What changed and why
You weren't actually saying no — you were flagging a capacity conflict. The rewrite makes that explicit, removes the apology, and hands the CFO a clean decision. Same message, more authority.
