# Example 2 — Translate a Cryptic Message from the CEO (Mode B)

**Scenario (synthetic).** A senior manager receives a vague message from the CEO and isn't sure what's actually being asked.

---

## INPUT

**Message received:**
> "Let's circle back on the strategy piece when we have more clarity around the operating model."

**User asks:** "What does this actually mean and what should I do?"

---

## OUTPUT (what the skill returns)

### 1. What they probably mean (plain English)
The CEO isn't ready to discuss your strategy work yet because a bigger decision — how the business will be structured (the "operating model") — hasn't been made. They're parking your piece, not rejecting it.

### 2. What they're not saying (subtext)
The operating-model question sits above your strategy work in priority and likely involves restructure or reporting-line decisions at their level. "Circle back" is a soft hold, not a commitment to a date. There may be uncertainty they can't yet share.

### 3. What they want from you
- Don't push the strategy piece right now.
- Keep it ready so you can move fast once the operating model lands.
- Signal that you understand the dependency, without chasing.

### 4. Suggested response
Hi [CEO],
Understood — I'll hold the strategy piece and keep it ready to go the moment the operating model is clearer. If it's useful, I can prepare two versions mapped to the likely structures, so we can move quickly when the time comes. Happy to leave it with you to flag when you're ready.
Cheers, [Name]
